## Issues that kill conversion

**1. No hero CTA**
The single most impactful growth fix on this page. No button above the fold means visitors have no directed action. Even a simple "Let's Talk →" link in the hero footer would materially improve contact rate. This is not a design preference — it's a conversion fundamental.

**2. High-friction contact form**
Five fields including a free-text "Context" textarea. For a first-touch inquiry, this is significant work. Visitors who don't already know Tarang will drop here. Options:
- Reduce to 3 fields (name, email, message) with the dropdown optional
- Or add a lower-commitment option: a direct email address alongside the form
- "No intake forms" copy + a multi-field intake form is a message inconsistency that a savvy visitor will notice and feel mildly manipulated by

**3. Anonymous testimonials**
Social proof with no verifiable source is negative social proof. A political consulting audience is highly skeptical and well-networked. "Senior Leadership — Political Consulting" as attribution signals either that Tarang couldn't get permission to use names (why?) or that these are not real quotes. Either interpretation is bad. Remove or replace.

**4. No direct contact method**
There is no email address, no phone number, no LinkedIn. The only contact path is the form. For a high-trust, relationship-driven category like political consulting, this signals inaccessibility. The target client is senior party leadership — people who call, not fill forms. At minimum, add an email address.

**5. "By referral and direct inquiry" availability**
This is actually good positioning — it signals exclusivity. But it's buried in the contact section's left column where almost nobody scrolls to read it. This should be in the hero or about section as a credibility signal.

**6. Value prop specificity**
The site says what Tarang does (research, programs, exec support). It does not say *for whom* or *with what results*. "Leadership walks into rooms informed" — which rooms? What decisions? One specific anonymised example ("In the run-up to [election type], delivered a research brief in 72 hours that shaped the party's [X] position") would do more conversion work than the entire about section.

---

## What would improve conversion most (in order)

1. Add hero CTA button — immediate
2. Add direct email address — immediate
3. Replace or remove testimonials — before launch
4. Reduce form to 3 fields — before launch
5. Add one concrete outcome to the about section — before launch
6. Move "by referral" signal upward — P1

---

## Verdict

This site will work well for people who already know Tarang and are coming to verify. It will fail for cold traffic or warm-but-unsure visitors. The design creates trust; the missing conversion infrastructure lets it leak out. Three fixes — hero CTA, direct email, real testimonials — would double the contact rate.
