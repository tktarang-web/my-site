# Panel Discussion — Tarang Kulkarni Site Review

*After individual reviews, the panel reconvened to compare notes and reach consensus on priorities.*

---

## Where we all agreed immediately

**The missing hero CTA was unanimous.**
Marcus flagged it as a P0 design failure. Ankit called it the single highest-impact conversion fix. Meera said "I scrolled immediately because there was nothing else to do." Rohit didn't mention it directly but noted the contact infrastructure is broken. This is the clearest fix on the page — one button, above the fold.

**The testimonials are doing active harm.**
All five of us flagged this independently. Shreya said they "damage credibility more than no testimonials." Ankit called them "negative social proof." Meera said she immediately thought "are these real?" Rohit noted the attribution is weaker than even what's standard in politically sensitive industries. This is a launch blocker — not because testimonials are required, but because *bad* testimonials are worse than none.

**"3 Core Capabilities" is an embarrassment.**
Marcus, Ankit, and Shreya all flagged this independently. It counts three things on the same page as if it were a credential. It should either be replaced with a real metric or removed.

---

## Where we had a substantive disagreement

**On the form friction:**
Ankit wanted to reduce the form to 3 fields. Meera pushed back — she said for her, the context field is actually useful because she'd want to provide context before a first conversation. Rohit agreed with Meera: "A 5-field form signals seriousness, not bureaucracy, for a high-touch service." The resolution: keep the fields but add a direct email address as an alternative path. The form isn't the problem — the lack of alternatives is.

**On specificity in the copy:**
Rohit and Meera both wanted named outcomes, specific elections, verifiable context. Shreya partially agreed but noted that in Indian political consulting, full discretion is often a professional requirement, not a choice. She said: "The site doesn't have to name the party — it needs to name the *scale and outcome* without the party." Ankit agreed: "One sentence like 'research delivered in 72 hours for a state-level election' does more than three paragraphs of capability claims." Resolution: Tarang needs to decide what he can and can't say, then say the maximum permissible.

**On the positioning ambiguity (founder vs. employee vs. consultant):**
Rohit called it a flag. Meera said it's standard in political circles to be vague about firm affiliations — she's not bothered. Marcus said the design reads "principal" not "employee" so if that's wrong, it needs fixing. Shreya said the site functions as a personal brand regardless of employment structure, and that's not inherently misleading. Resolution: Tarang needs to consciously choose how he presents — not leave it to interpretation.

---

## Themes that cut across every review

**1. The gap between looking credible and being verifiable.**
The design creates an impression of seriousness and credibility. The content doesn't provide enough to verify that impression. For a cold visitor, this gap is enough to close the tab. For a warm referral, it's enough to confirm the meeting. The site is currently optimised for the second scenario and underperforming on the first.

**2. The copy is good but not specific.**
"No fluff, no disclaimers, just outcomes" — everyone agreed this was well-written. Everyone also agreed it could apply to any competent consultant. The distinctiveness is in the framing and voice, not in the substance. Specificity is the fix — not a rewrite.

**3. "Executive Assistancy" needs to change.**
Shreya, Meera, and Rohit all noted it's not standard terminology. Ankit noted it's likely not searched for. The replacement depends on what Tarang actually does in this role — "Chief of Staff Services," "Senior EA," or "Principal's Office Support" would all be more legible.

**4. The contact section sends conflicting signals.**
"Direct line. No intake forms." is a strong positioning statement. The five-field form that follows undermines it. The fix is either changing the heading copy or adding a direct email address — ideally both.

---

## Consensus priorities going into the fix list

1. Hero CTA — add it, non-negotiable
2. Testimonials — fix or remove before launch
3. Direct contact method (email) — add alongside the form
4. "3 Core Capabilities" stat — replace or remove
5. "No intake forms" copy contradiction — fix the heading
6. "Executive Assistancy" terminology — rename
7. Positioning clarity (founder/employee/consultant) — Tarang's call, but make it a conscious one
8. Specificity — one concrete proof point, even anonymised, in the About section

---

## Final note from the panel

The site is genuinely well-made. The design is above average for this category. The copy has real personality. The problems are fixable without a rebuild — most of them are one paragraph, one button, or one decision. The risk is launching with the current credibility gaps and having the first wave of visitors leave unconvinced by something that could have been fixed in an afternoon.
