# Shreya Iyer — Brand Strategist Review

---

## First impression

The design has taste. The typography is confident. But the moment I read the copy, I feel like I've read it before — not on *this* site, but on every polished consultant site that went through a copywriting template. That's the core problem. The aesthetics are doing more work than the words, and on a personal brand site, it should be the other way around.

---

## What's working

- "Your campaign runs. Your decisions land." — strong opening. Two short sentences, parallel structure, client-outcome framing. This is the best line on the site.
- The voice is direct and the fragment style is consistent. No corporate filler.
- "The program doesn't stall because someone isn't managing it — it runs because someone is." — this line has personality.

---

## What's not

**1. I don't know who Tarang is.**
The About section tells me his credentials (MBA, IIFT, zero-to-one) and his offer (research, programs, exec support). It does not tell me anything about *him*. What does he believe about political work that others don't? What's the philosophy? What has he seen that shaped how he operates? There is no worldview here. No point of view. Just a capability list dressed up in good typography.

**2. "Zero-to-one firm-building" is startup jargon in a political context.**
His audience is senior party leadership — people who built constituency networks from scratch over 20 years. "Zero-to-one" is a Silicon Valley frame. It will land wrong or land as nothing. Replace it with something that means something in their world.

**3. The stats actively weaken the brand.**
"3 — Core Capabilities." This just counts the services on the same page. It communicates nothing. It fills a box. The stat grid promises data and delivers decoration. Either replace with real numbers (elections worked, years in the field, research briefs delivered) or remove entirely.

**4. The testimonials are fabricated-feeling.**
"Senior Leadership — Political Consulting." That's not a testimonial. That's a placeholder wearing a testimonial's clothes. Anyone who lands on this site and reads that will feel the inauthenticity immediately. It doesn't just fail to build trust — it actively erodes it. Two anonymous quotes from unnamed people at unnamed organizations is worse than no testimonials.

**5. "Executive Assistancy" is not a word.**
It's not how anyone in Indian political circles describes this function. The equivalent is "Chief of Staff" or "Senior EA" or "Principal's Office." Using invented terminology makes the reader work harder, and they won't — they'll just move on.

**6. The brand has no memory hook.**
If I close this tab and someone asks me what that site was about in 5 minutes, I'll say "political consultant, good design, execution-focused." That's accurate but not memorable. There's no story, no specific claim, no image that sticks. Distinctive brands have *one thing* that lodges. This site doesn't have it yet.

---

## Verdict

The copy is better than average. The design is significantly better than average. But this is a personal brand site for someone in a high-trust, relationship-driven industry where differentiation is everything — and nothing here differentiates Tarang from any other well-educated, experienced operator who is "execution-focused." The aesthetic is doing the work that the substance should be doing. Fix the testimonials, kill the "3 Core Capabilities" stat, find one specific thing that makes Tarang the *only* choice for a certain type of client, and build the copy around that.
